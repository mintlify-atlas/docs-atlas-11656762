---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Get from zero to your first successful data sync in minutes."
---

This guide walks you through setting up Airbyte and running your first sync. Choose between Airbyte Cloud (no infrastructure to manage) or self-hosted via Docker.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Airbyte Cloud">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Sign up for Airbyte Cloud">
        Go to [cloud.airbyte.io/signup](https://cloud.airbyte.io/signup) and create an account. You receive free credits to get started — no credit card required.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a source">
        From the left sidebar, click **Sources** then **New source**. Search for and select a connector (for example, GitHub or Postgres). Fill in the required configuration fields and click **Set up source**.

        Airbyte runs a connection check to verify the credentials before saving.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a destination">
        Click **Destinations** then **New destination**. Select a destination connector (for example, BigQuery or Snowflake). Provide the connection details and click **Set up destination**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a connection">
        Click **Connections** then **New connection**. Select the source and destination you just created.

        Configure the sync:
        - **Sync frequency** — how often Airbyte runs the sync (e.g., every 24 hours)
        - **Streams** — which tables or data streams to replicate
        - **Sync mode** — Full Refresh or Incremental per stream

        Click **Set up connection**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Trigger your first sync">
        On the connection overview page, click **Sync now**. Monitor progress in the **Job history** tab. When the status shows **Succeeded**, your data has landed in the destination.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Self-hosted (Docker)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Prerequisites">
        Make sure the following are installed on your machine:

        - [Docker](https://docs.docker.com/get-docker/) (v24 or later)
        - [Docker Compose](https://docs.docker.com/compose/install/) (v2 or later)
        - At least 4 GB RAM allocated to Docker
      </Step>

      <Step title="Download the Airbyte deployment files">
        ```bash
        git clone --depth=1 https://github.com/airbytehq/airbyte.git
        cd airbyte
        ```
      </Step>

      <Step title="Start Airbyte">
        ```bash
        docker compose up
        ```

        On first run, Docker pulls the required images. This may take a few minutes. When all services are healthy, you will see log output from the `airbyte-server`, `airbyte-worker`, and `airbyte-webapp` containers.

        <Note>
          To run in the background, append the `-d` flag: `docker compose up -d`.
        </Note>
      </Step>

      <Step title="Open the UI">
        Navigate to [http://localhost:8000](http://localhost:8000) in your browser.

        The default credentials are:
        - **Username:** `airbyte`
        - **Password:** `password`

        <Warning>
          Change these credentials before exposing Airbyte to any network. Update the `BASIC_AUTH_USERNAME` and `BASIC_AUTH_PASSWORD` environment variables in your `.env` file.
        </Warning>
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a source">
        From the left sidebar, click **Sources** then **New source**. Search for and select a connector. Fill in the required fields and click **Set up source**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a destination">
        Click **Destinations** then **New destination**. Select a connector, provide connection details, and click **Set up destination**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Create a connection">
        Click **Connections** then **New connection**. Select the source and destination, configure stream selection and sync modes, then click **Set up connection**.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Trigger your first sync">
        On the connection page, click **Sync now**. Check the **Job history** tab to monitor progress. A **Succeeded** status confirms data has been written to your destination.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
